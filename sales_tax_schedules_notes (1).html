3RD SCHEDULE Tax on Retail Price (MRP)
"Tax is on the price printed on the packet — not on what the seller charges"
Section 3(2)(a) · Definition of "retail price" in Section 2(27)
The Rule Goods in this schedule are taxed at 18% of the retail price printed on the pack. The manufacturer (or importer for imported goods) must print the MRP prominently on each packet. Retail price = price fixed by manufacturer inclusive of all duties/charges except income tax & sales tax itself. For imported goods: MRP must not be less than 130% of customs value.
EXAM TRAP If a 3rd Schedule good is ALSO in the 8th Schedule, the 8th Schedule rate applies to the retail price (not 18%). The base is always retail price; only the rate changes.
EXAM TRAP — Chilling deduction For aerated water, beverages, mineral water, and fruit juices: reduction for chilling charges cannot exceed 5% of the retail price.

6TH SCHEDULE Full Exemption (No Tax)
"No tax at all — but also no input tax credit"
Section 13(1) · Three Tables: Table-1 (Imports/Supplies), Table-2 (Special cases), Table-3 (Machinery)
The Rule Goods listed here are completely exempt from sales tax. Unlike zero-rating (5th Schedule), the supplier of exempt goods CANNOT claim input tax credit on purchases used to make those exempt supplies. Federal Government can grant emergency exemptions under Section 13(2) by notification.
EXAM TRAP — Branded vs Unbranded Spices (red chillies, ginger, turmeric), if sold under a brand name or trademark, lose their exemption and fall into the 3rd Schedule (MRP basis). Without a brand = 6th Schedule (exempt). This is a favourite past-paper distinction.
EXAM TRAP — PATA Region Goods purchased from suppliers in PATA are generally outside the sales tax net. In past papers, ginger bought in bulk from a PATA wholesaler is treated as unbranded/exempt (6th Schedule), while the same ginger under a brand name is taxable.

8TH SCHEDULE Reduced Rate Tax
"Still taxable, but at a rate below 18%"
Section 3(2)(aa) · Tax base = value of supply (or retail price if also in 3rd Schedule)
The Rule Goods listed here are taxable but at special reduced rates ranging from 1% to 18%. The normal rules for tax invoicing, input tax (subject to conditions), and registration all apply. Many items carry special conditions — always read column (5) of the Schedule.
CRITICAL — Input Tax on Agricultural Machinery (7%) Under Section 8(1)(k), input tax on agricultural machinery purchased at 7% under the 8th Schedule is NOT allowed as an input tax credit.
CRITICAL — Drugs/Medicines at 1% Tax charged and deposited by the manufacturer or importer is final discharge in the supply chain. No further tax at wholesale or retail stage. No input tax adjustment allowed anywhere in the supply chain.

11TH SCHEDULE Sales Tax Withholding
"The buyer deducts a portion of the tax and deposits it directly with FBR"
Section 3(7) & 3(7A) · Inserted by Finance Act 2019
The Rule Designated withholding agents deduct sales tax (or a portion of it) from the payment to suppliers and deposit it with the government. The supplier still issues a full tax invoice. The buyer deducts the amount as specified and pays the balance to the supplier.
IMPORTANT — Withholding does NOT apply to Electrical energy; natural gas; petroleum products (from exploration companies, refineries, OMCs, dealers of motor spirit/HSD); vegetable ghee & cooking oil; telecom services; 3rd Schedule MRP goods; supplies by importers who already paid value addition tax at import; supplies by an Active Taxpayer to another registered person (with exceptions for S.Nos. 5, 7, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13).

12TH SCHEDULE Value Addition Tax at Import (3%)
"An extra 3% charged at the customs stage on top of normal 18% — minimum value addition presumed"
Section 7A(2) · Inserted by Finance Act 2019 · Also called "minimum value addition tax" or "VAT at import"
The Rule ALL taxable imported goods are subject to an additional 3% ad valorem at the import stage, over and above the normal sales tax rate. This is charged IN ADDITION to the regular sales tax (not instead of it). Purpose: to collect tax equivalent to the value addition that importers typically make before selling.
EXAM FOCUS — Learn the EXCLUSIONS (these come in exams) The 12th Schedule 3% does NOT apply to the goods listed below. If any of these appear in an exam scenario involving imports, do NOT add the 3%.
